This summer, the biggest sporting event on the planet arrives in North America. The 2026 FIFA World Cup begins on June 11 in Mexico City and runs through July 19, when the world's best teams will competeTo take part in a contest trying to win. for the most covetedStrongly desired by many people; something everyone wants. trophy in football. But beyond the stadiums and the statistics, one question dominatesIs the most important or powerful thing in a situation. every conversation: will Lionel Messi play?

Messi is 38 years old. He turns 39 on June 24 — right in the middle of Argentina's group stage. He has played in five World Cups already, starting in Germany in 2006 when he was just 18. He holds the all-time record for World Cup appearancesThe number of times a player has played in official matches. — 26 games — surpassing Germany's Lothar Matthäus in 2022. And in Qatar that year, he finally lifted the one trophyA prize given to the winner of a competition, usually a cup or statue. that had escaped him his entire career.

"I would love to be there. I'm going to assess that day by day and see if I can really be 100 per cent."

— Lionel Messi, on the 2026 World Cup

His coach, Lionel Scaloni, is doing everything he can to make it happen. This week, Messi was namedSelected and officially included in a team or list. in Argentina's squad for friendly matches against Mauritania and Zambia at La Bombonera in Buenos Aires. Scaloni confirmed Messi will play in both games — his last chance to be assessed before the squadThe group of players selected to represent a team in a competition. is finalised for the tournament.

"I believe that, for the good of football, he should be there. I will do everything possible to make sure he is."

— Lionel Scaloni, Argentina coach

But the World Cup in 2026 is historic for reasons far beyond Messi. This edition is the largest in the tournament's 96-year history. For the first time ever, 48 teamsThe total number of national teams competing — up from 32 in 2022. will participate, divided into 12 groups of four. That means 104 matches in total — 40 more than in Qatar. The tournament is also the first ever to be hostedOrganised and held in a particular place or country. by three countries at the same time: the United States, Canada and Mexico.

2026 World Cup — Key Facts
  • Dates: June 11 – July 19, 2026
  • Hosts: USA (11 cities), Mexico (3 cities), Canada (2 cities) — first ever 3-nation host
  • Teams: 48 teams — up from 32 in Qatar 2022
  • Matches: 104 total — the most in World Cup history
  • Opening match: Mexico vs. opponent at Estadio Azteca, Mexico City (June 11)
  • Final: MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey (July 19)
  • Official ball: Adidas Trionda — inspired by the three host nations
  • Argentina's group: Group J — vs Algeria (June 16), Austria (June 22), Jordan (June 27)

Sixteen cities across three countries will host matches. The finalThe last and most important match of a tournament, where the winner is decided. will be played at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey — the largest stadium in the United States, with a capacity of 82,500 people. The opening ceremony will take place at the legendary Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, the only stadium in the world to have hosted three World Cup tournaments.

Argentina, the defending championsThe team that won the previous tournament and is trying to win again. after their unforgettable victory in Qatar 2022, begin their campaignA series of organised efforts to achieve a goal — in sport, the full run of matches in a tournament. on June 16 against Algeria in Kansas City. Whether Messi is wearing the number 10 shirt that day is still uncertain. But the football world — from Buenos Aires to Bangkok — is hoping the answer is yes.

"It's something extraordinary to be able to be in a World Cup. I would like to be there, to be well and be an important part of helping my team."

— Lionel Messi

One thing is certain: whether Messi plays or watches from the stands, the 2026 World Cup will be a summer unlike any other. The biggest show on earth is coming to North America — and the countdown has already begun.
