You heard the engine first. A deep, raw, unmistakable roar — the sound of a Formula 1 car running at full power. Then came the smokeTyre smoke — created when a car spins its wheels at high speed on the road., rising in thick white clouds above Palermo. And then, from every direction, came the crowd. Six hundred thousand people. On the streets of Buenos Aires. On a Sunday afternoon. All of them there for one reason: Franco Colapinto had brought Formula 1 home.

On April 26, 2026, the road showA public demonstration event where a racing driver performs in a car on city streets. officially called "Mercado Libre presents: Franco Colapinto Road Show Buenos Aires 2026" transformed Palermo into the largest motorsport event Argentina had seen in decades. Along a specially built 2-kilometre circuitA track or route used for racing or demonstration driving. on Avenida del Libertador and Avenida Sarmiento, Colapinto drove a 2012 Lotus E20 — painted in Alpine blue — at full speed, performing donuts, full-throttle runs and crowd lapsOne complete trip around a racing circuit or route. that left the entire city breathless.

But the event was more than just a show. Colapinto also drove a replicaAn exact copy of something — made to look identical to the original. of the Mercedes W196 — the car in which Juan Manuel Fangio won his 1954 and 1955 Formula 1 World Championships. It was a moment loaded with meaning: Argentina's greatest sporting legend, honoured by Argentina's newest hero. Colapinto paused at several points around the route to acknowledge the crowd — standing on the car, waving, smiling — before completing a final lap on a support truck through the thousands of fans who had lined the streets for hours.

"Wow, what an amazing feeling to be able to drive at home and especially to enjoy the day with so many fans. I love Argentinians. We are the best fans in the world."

— Franco Colapinto, after the Road Show · April 26, 2026

The event was sponsoredFinancially supported by a company in exchange for publicity and association with the event. by Mercado Libre — one of Latin America's largest tech companies — and organised by Alpine, Colapinto's Formula 1 team. The crowd of 600,000 made it one of the largest Formula 1 demonstrationsA public display or event showing something — in this case, a car performing on streets. ever held anywhere in the world. To put that in perspective: the entire population of Rosario, Argentina's third-largest city, could have fit in that crowd twice.

But beyond the spectacle, Colapinto used the moment to make a much larger point. Argentina has not hosted a Formula 1 Grand Prix since 1998 — 28 years ago. The Oscar y Juan Gálvez Autodrome in Buenos Aires, which last held an F1 race that year, is already undergoingIn the process of experiencing or going through something — usually a change or development. redevelopment and is set to host MotoGP from 2027. The dream — shared by Colapinto, millions of Argentine fans and Formula 1 itself — is that a Grand Prix could follow.

"We are showing Formula 1 that we deserve to return to the calendar. We deserve to have a race again."

— Franco Colapinto, Buenos Aires · April 26, 2026
Franco Colapinto — who is he?
  • Born: May 27, 2003 in Pilar, Buenos Aires province
  • Team: Alpine F1 · partner: Pierre Gasly
  • F1 debut: 2024 Italian Grand Prix (Monza) with Williams — first Argentine in F1 for 23 years
  • 2025: Joined Alpine as reserve driver · replaced Jack Doohan from round 7 onwards
  • 2026: Confirmed as full Alpine race driver — scored his first points with the team in China (10th place)
  • Next race: Miami Grand Prix — May 2, 2026
  • Hero connection: Also drove a replica of Fangio's 1954–55 championship-winning Mercedes W196 at the event

For now, Colapinto heads to Miami for the next Formula 1 race — the sixth round of the 2026 season. His 2026 has been a season of learning: strong race paceThe consistent speed a driver maintains during a race. but still working on his qualifying speed on Saturdays, which has left him starting further back than he would like. His teammate Gasly has outqualified him in all three races so far. But in the Sunday races, Colapinto has shown the raw talent that made Argentina fall in love with him in the first place.

Whatever happens on the track, the image of April 26 will not fade easily: 600,000 people in the streets of Buenos Aires, watching a 22-year-old kid from Pilar make the sound of a Formula 1 engine echo down Avenida del Libertador for the first time in a generation. A dream. A statement. And perhaps — just perhaps — the beginning of Argentina's return to the Formula 1 calendar.
